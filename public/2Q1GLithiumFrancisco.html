Ateneo de Manila University

Short History

Ateneo de Manila University was founded in 1859 by the Society of Jesus (Jesuits). Originally located in Intramuros, it began as the Escuela Municipal de Manila, which was the only primary school in the city at the time. In 1865, it was renamed Ateneo Municipal de Manila, and in the same year, secondary education was introduced. When American colonial rule began in 1902, the Ateneo Municipal lost its government subsidy.

By 1908, the government recognized it as a college authorized to offer bachelor’s degrees and certificates in various fields, including electrical engineering. A year later, in 1909, the Jesuits officially removed the word “Municipal” from its name, and it has since been known as Ateneo de Manila. American Jesuits took administration in 1921, as a result of the Spanish being "exiled".

During World War II, much of Ateneo was destroyed, with only one structure surviving, the statue of St. Joseph and the Child Jesus. After the war, the remaining structures were salvaged, and in 1952, the campus was rebuilt in Loyola Heights, which is now the largest of the four campuses, being 83 hectars big, ranking as the 8th largest university in the Philippines. Over time, three additional campuses were established within the National Capital Region (NCR).

Core Values

Ateneo’s education is built around Jesuit principles that shape its students into compassionate leaders and responsible citizens. Its core values include:

  • Magis: Striving for excellence and doing more for others(WIP).
  • Cura Personalis: Care for the whole person, recognizing individual dignity and growth(WIP).
  • Ad Majorem Dei Gloriam (AMDG): Doing all things for the greater glory of God(WIP).
  • Men and Women for Others: A commitment to serve others and contribute to nation-building. This Core Value is mainly for Ateneo de Manila University students, and isn't taught to students under AGS, AJHS, ASHS (WIP).
  • 7C's: The 7 C's stand for Christ Centered, Competence, Conscience, Character, Compassion, Community, and Culture. Every Atenean, from Kinder to University Student must have these characteristics/valu

Campuses

Ateneo operates several campuses across Metro Manila:

  • Loyola Heights Campus: The main campus, housing most undergraduate and graduate programs.
  • Rockwell Campus: Home to the Ateneo Graduate School of Business.
  • Salcedo Campus: Focused on continuing education and professional development.
  • Pasig Campus: The Ateneo School of Medicine and Public Health.
